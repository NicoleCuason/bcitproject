KIMBAP

a popular Korean dish made with steamed rice, vegetables, meat (such as beef, egg, or fish), and pickled ingredients, all wrapped in a sheet of nori (seaweed)

SUNDUBU-JIGAE

a Korean stew made with soft silken tofu, vegetables, garlic, gochujang (Korean chili paste), and optional meat or seafood, often served with a raw egg added just before serving.

PAJEON

a savory Korean pancake made with a simple batter of flour, water, and egg, mixed with scallions (green onions), and often includes vegetables, seafood, or meat

BULGOGI

a popular Korean dish made with thinly sliced beef, marinated in a sweet and savory mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, sugar, and pepper

JAPCHAE

a Korean stir-fried dish made with sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon), mixed with sautéed vegetables like spinach, carrots, mushrooms, and onions, and often accompanied by thinly sliced beef or other proteins

BIBIMBAP

a traditional Korean dish consisting of a bowl of steamed rice topped with a variety of sautéed and seasoned vegetables (such as spinach, carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms), a fried egg, and often gochujang (Korean chili paste) for flavor

DAKBOKKEUMTANG

a hearty and flavorful dish made with chicken, potatoes, carrots, onions, and sometimes other vegetables, all simmered in a spicy and savory broth

KIMCHI BOKKEUMBAP

a popular Korean comfort food made by stir-frying cooked rice with kimchi, vegetables, and sometimes meat (like pork or beef)

SAMGYUPSAL

a popular Korean dish consisting of thick slices of pork belly that are grilled at the table

YANGNYEOM CHICKEN

a popular Korean dish known for its crispy, double-fried coating and flavorful, often spicy-sweet sauce

MANDU

steamed or fried dumplings filled with a mixture of ground meat, vegetables, and tofu, served with a soy-based dipping sauce

BINGSU

a popular Korean shaved ice dessert, consists of sweetened condensed milk, red bean paste, fruits, chewy rice cakes, or ice cream
